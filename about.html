Our Cosmic Home

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy spanning approximately 100,000 light-years in diameter. It contains between 100-400 billion stars and at least that many planets. Our solar system resides in one of the galaxy's spiral arms, about 27,000 light-years from the galactic center.

Key Features:

  • Age: ~13.6 billion years
  • Contains a supermassive black hole (Sagittarius A*) at its center
  • Part of the Local Group of galaxies
  • Rotates at about 828,000 km/h

The galaxy's name comes from its appearance as a dim glowing band arching across the night sky, resembling spilled milk. Through telescopes, we can observe its complex structure of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter.
